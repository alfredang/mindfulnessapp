This is an awareness
of breath meditation. Regular practice of this meditation
cultivates awareness, concentration,
and calmness. Settling into a
sitting posture that is both steady and
comfortable, either on a chair or on a
cushion on the floor. If sitting in a chair, both feet are
on the floor. sensing and
feeling the parts of the body that
are supported by the floor,
cushion, or chair. The lower body
is grounded, and the spine
and back rise up, not rigid, yet elevated, shoulders resting
softly on your sides. Eyes can be
closed or lowered. Face and mouth
are relaxed. Turning attention
now to the breath. Feeling the breathing
in the body. Are you inhaling or
are you exhaling? Seeing if you can
****** the breath as it moves in and
out of the body. Feeling a sense
of expansion and contraction in the
body as you breathe. No need to
control or change the breath in any way. Simply being
with the breath. noticing if your mind has wandered off to
thoughts of the past or thoughts
of the future this is completely normal there is no need
to judge yourself just gently and
firmly bring your attention
back to the breath inhaling and exhaling Perhaps being curious, where do you
feel the breath most vividly in the body? Is it when the air
enters the nostrils or the rise and fall of
the chest or abdomen? Allowing your
attention to settle on where
you feel the breath most vividly
in your body. Resting attention.
on the breath. The breath is an anchor to the present moment. We cannot breathe
in the past. We cannot breathe
in the future. We can only breathe
in this moment. You can return to the breath again and again as a home base or
a steady guide. As the bell
rings to signal the end to this
meditation, stay present with
the breath until you can no longer hear
the sound of the bell. Checking in to
see how you feel and transitioning with awareness into
your next activity.
